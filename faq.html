// How it works

ArtDrop runs five steps automatically:

  • Analyze — AI classifies the image: style, colors, mood, composition
  • Write — title, description, and SEO tags generated from the analysis
  • Upload — high-res file goes to your Backblaze B2 bucket
  • Create — one product per template at each print provider you've configured
  • Publish — all products published to the right categories in your store with alt text, tags, and SEO

A typical drop takes 3–8 minutes end to end, depending on how many product types you have configured. More templates = more products = slightly more time. You can watch every step in real time in the app's live log — it shows you exactly what's happening at each stage.

Better than most humans writing product descriptions under time pressure. The AI does a two-pass analysis — it classifies the image first, then writes from the structural data, not generic impressions. This produces copy that's specific to what's actually in the piece rather than vague "vibrant colors and dynamic composition" boilerplate.

You can always edit any description in Shopify after it's published. But most will be publish-ready as-is.

You can edit any product description directly inside ArtDrop before publishing. If the result misses the mark, there's also a Regenerate button that runs the AI again on the same image. You always review before anything goes live — nothing publishes automatically without your approval.

Use the built-in Voice Trainer. Drop an image, see the AI-generated copy, and react in plain English — "too flowery", "shorter sentences", "never say whimsical." ArtDrop refines the copy based on your feedback, shows you a side-by-side comparison, and keeps going until it sounds like you. Then it extracts concrete rules from the conversation and saves them automatically.

No prompt engineering, no rule-writing, no guessing. Just react to what you see — ArtDrop learns your style from the conversation. You can train on multiple images to make sure the voice holds across different pieces. Each training session costs about $0.03-0.05 in API fees.

Yes. You configure your own product templates in ArtDrop — each template is one product format (a specific size poster, a canvas, a phone case, etc.). Every file you drop will create one product per active template. Add or remove templates any time; changes take effect immediately.

One artist might run two templates and get two products per drop. Another might have fifteen templates. ArtDrop doesn't decide — you do.

PNG JPG JPEG TIF TIFF

16-bit TIFs work. File size doesn't matter — 4MB or 1.5GB, ArtDrop handles it. Your high-res master goes to Backblaze, and ArtDrop automatically generates the right size for each print provider. Resolution recommendation: 300 DPI at your largest print size. For a 24×36" poster that's 7200×10800px minimum.

If your files contain EXIF or XMP metadata — camera, lens, focal length, aperture, shutter speed, ISO — ArtDrop can extract it and use it in three ways:

  • Full display — tech specs shown on your product pages (great for photographers who want buyers to see the craft behind the shot)
  • Tags only — metadata extracted and added as product tags for SEO and filtering, but not shown on the page
  • Off — metadata ignored completely

This is configurable in your settings. Photographers love it. Painters and illustrators can turn it off — their files typically don't contain this data anyway, so nothing changes for them.
